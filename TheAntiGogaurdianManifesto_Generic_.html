The Anti-Goguardian Manifesto

Preamble

        We as the philosophers of the student body have determined that certain teachers are misusing their power over students’ use of their web browsing devices. Said teachers have committed atrocities such as closing students tabs without valid reason, blocking students’ access to sites though they are causing no problems in class, and restricting students’ access without reason. These are but a mere few of the misdoings of these unjust instructors.

  1. The Disadvantages of GoGuardian

We believe Gogaurdian has its place, but we disagree fundamentally with its principles. Gogaurdian should not be used to keep students off of every site, but rather it should be used to block the people who are causing problems or disruptions in class. If students are not impeding on other students’ opportunities to learn, it is their problem. For if a student is using their chromebook to disrupt class, it is a problem for everyone, whereas if they are quietly using it, not disrupting class. If a student is distracted by the chromebook, and yet not disrupting others’ opportunities to learn, it is their fault not the teachers’.

  1. The Abuse of Power

        The teachers are abusing their power. If a student needs to use something which may not be directly related to the class, and they are finished with their required work, the teachers should not block them, and yet they do. Some teachers, namely Wyatt Ammon, enjoy closing students’ tabs for fun, and to annoy them. Such actions are not appreciated by us, the students, as we feel we need the opportunity to work on things not school related if we have completed the assignment, and the teacher has no more required work for us to do.

  1. Blanket Bans

        Blanket bans are not a good thing. Some teachers will have it set up so all sites with the exception of a few whitelisted sites are blocked. This often restricts students access to sites which are extremely useful for class and other school related matters. One example of this is blocking a tool like Gmail. Gmail allows us students to have communication with our teachers and our parents if you need. For example, sometimes a schedule changes and a student needs to tell their parents when to pick them up. If Gmail is blocked, we have no way to do that.
